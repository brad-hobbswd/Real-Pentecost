Acts 2:38

Acts 2:38

The apostolic response to the gospel of Jesus Christ and the continuing promise given to every generation.

"Then Peter said unto them, Repent, and be baptized every one of you in the name of Jesus Christ for the remission of sins, and ye shall receive the gift of the Holy Ghost."

Acts 2:38 stands at the heart of the Day of Pentecost. It is the inspired response given by the apostle Peter after the multitude asked a sincere and searching question:

"Men and brethren, what shall we do?"

Peter did not offer human tradition, philosophical ideas, or personal opinion. Under the inspiration of the Holy Ghost, he proclaimed the apostolic pattern that would define the message of the early church.

The verse presents three inseparable elements: repentance, baptism in the name of Jesus Christ, and the gift of the Holy Ghost. Together they reveal God's gracious invitation into a transformed life centered upon Jesus Christ.

Repent

Repentance is the beginning of every genuine relationship with God. It is more than sorrow for sin; it is a change of heart and mind that turns completely toward Jesus Christ.

Throughout Scripture, God calls His people to repentance because He desires restoration rather than condemnation. Pentecost begins with a heart willing to surrender completely to His will.

Baptized in the Name of Jesus Christ

Peter instructed every believer to be baptized in the name of Jesus Christ for the remission of sins. Baptism is not presented as a human tradition but as an act of obedient faith, identifying the believer with the death, burial, and resurrection of Christ.

Throughout the book of Acts, believers responded to the gospel by being baptized in the name of Jesus, demonstrating the authority and saving power found in His name alone.

The Gift of the Holy Ghost

Pentecost did not end with repentance and baptism. Peter declared that those who obeyed the gospel would receive the gift of the Holy Ghost, God's abiding presence dwelling within His people.

The Holy Ghost empowers believers to walk in holiness, grow in truth, and faithfully witness to the saving work of Jesus Christ. What began in the upper room continues in every generation that responds to God's invitation.

"For the promise is unto you, and to your children, and to all that are afar off, even as many as the Lord our God shall call."

Acts 2:39 (KJV)

Acts 2:38 is not merely a historical statement. It is the continuing apostolic invitation to every generation: repent, be baptized in the name of Jesus Christ, and receive the gift of the Holy Ghost. The promise remains rooted in the faithfulness of God and the saving work of Jesus Christ.
