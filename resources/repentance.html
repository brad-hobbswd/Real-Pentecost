Acts 2:38

Repentance

Turning toward Jesus Christ and embracing the new life God graciously offers.

"Then Peter said unto them, Repent, and be baptized every one of you in the name of Jesus Christ for the remission of sins, and ye shall receive the gift of the Holy Ghost."

Repentance is the beginning of the Christian life. It is God's loving invitation to turn away from sin and turn toward Jesus Christ with faith, humility, and wholehearted surrender.

Throughout Scripture, repentance is presented not as an act of despair but as the doorway to mercy, forgiveness, restoration, and hope. God calls His people because He desires to redeem rather than condemn.

On the Day of Pentecost, Peter's first instruction to those who believed the gospel was simple and direct: "Repent." That invitation continues today, calling every heart to experience the grace and transforming power of Jesus Christ.

A Change of Heart

Biblical repentance is more than feeling sorrow for sin. It is a change of heart and mind that produces a new direction in life. As we acknowledge our need for God's mercy, He begins the work of renewing us from the inside out.

Genuine repentance leads to joyful obedience, deeper faith, and a growing desire to walk with Jesus Christ every day.

The Kindness of God

Repentance begins with God's love. The Lord does not call people to Himself through fear alone but through His mercy, patience, and unfailing grace. Every invitation to repent is an invitation to experience forgiveness and a restored relationship with Him.

Scripture declares that God is "not willing that any should perish, but that all should come to repentance." His desire is to redeem, restore, and transform every heart that turns toward Him.

Fruit Worthy of Repentance

A repentant heart produces a changed life. As the Holy Ghost works within us, old habits give way to new desires, bitterness becomes forgiveness, pride is replaced with humility, and self-will yields to the lordship of Jesus Christ.

Repentance is not perfection but a continual turning toward God. Day by day, believers learn to trust His Word, follow His leading, and grow in the likeness of Christ.

The Beginning of a New Life

On the Day of Pentecost, repentance was the first step into a life transformed by God's grace. It prepared hearts for baptism in the name of Jesus Christ and the promise of the Holy Ghost, beginning a journey of steadfast faith and joyful obedience.

The call to repent continues today. Every person is invited to leave behind the old life and discover the peace, hope, and redemption found in Jesus Christ.

"Repent ye therefore, and be converted, that your sins may be blotted out, when the times of refreshing shall come from the presence of the Lord."

Acts 3:19 (KJV)

Repentance is not the end of the story—it is the beginning. It is the first step into a continuing life centered upon Jesus Christ, sustained by the Holy Ghost, grounded in the apostles' doctrine, and marked by worship, fellowship, prayer, holiness, and faithful witness.
