Prologue

Before the Wind

Before the sound from heaven, there was a promise waiting to be fulfilled.

Jerusalem was already awake.

The narrow streets echoed with footsteps as pilgrims made their way toward the temple, joining thousands who had traveled from distant provinces for the Feast of Weeks.

Merchants arranged baskets of grain and fruit beneath shaded awnings while children hurried beside parents carrying offerings that generations before them had carried to the same city.

The morning appeared wonderfully ordinary.

Roman soldiers watched the crowds from elevated stone walls with practiced indifference.

Priests prepared for another day of worship.

Friends embraced after long journeys.

Travelers exchanged stories in accents gathered from every corner of the empire.

Life moved with familiar certainty.

Only Weeks Earlier

Only a few weeks earlier the city had witnessed events impossible to forget.

A Galilean teacher had entered Jerusalem surrounded by shouting crowds.

Days later He was crucified outside the city gates.

Rumors of an empty tomb followed, along with astonishing reports that His disciples claimed to have seen Him alive.

The excitement had begun to settle.

Ordinary life always returns.

Above the Crowded Streets

Above the crowded streets, hidden from public attention, another group waited.

One hundred twenty believers remained together in quiet obedience to the final instruction Jesus had given before ascending into heaven.

Wait for the promise.

No one knew what the promise would look like.

No one expected the sound that would soon interrupt the city.

No one imagined that fishermen, former tax collectors, women, laborers, and ordinary disciples would become the beginning of a movement that would cross empires, survive persecution, outlive kingdoms, and carry the name of Jesus Christ to the ends of the earth.

The Dividing Line

History often changes without warning.

One moment appears no different from the thousands that came before.

The next becomes the dividing line by which every future generation measures time.

Pentecost was such a moment.
Not because heaven briefly touched earth.
Because heaven chose to remain.
