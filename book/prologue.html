Prologue

Before There Was an Upper Room

Every promise of God moves toward its fulfillment in Jesus Christ.

Before there was an upper room, there was a promise.

Long before the sound of a rushing mighty wind echoed through Jerusalem, prophets lifted their voices and declared that God would do something new. He would pour out His Spirit upon all flesh, give His people a new heart, and write His law upon lives transformed by His grace.

Generations waited for that day.

They watched kingdoms rise and fall. They endured exile, silence, disappointment, and longing. Yet through every season, the promises of God remained steadfast, pointing toward a Redeemer who would accomplish what no sacrifice, ceremony, or earthly kingdom ever could.

Then Jesus Christ appeared.

He walked the roads of Galilee, healed the broken, welcomed the forgotten, forgave sinners, and proclaimed that the kingdom of God was at hand. In Him the promises of the prophets began to unfold before the eyes of those willing to believe.

Yet even as His disciples followed Him, Jesus spoke of another promise still to come. They would be endued with power from on high. The Father would give the Holy Ghost, and ordinary men and women would become witnesses to the ends of the earth.

His crucifixion seemed to silence every expectation.

His resurrection declared that death itself had been defeated.

His ascension left His followers with one simple command:

Wait for the promise of the Father.

So they gathered together with one accord, praying, worshiping, and trusting the words of the One who had never failed them.

They could not have imagined that they stood at the edge of a moment that would forever change the world.

Yet Pentecost was never intended to become merely a historical milestone or a doctrine preserved in memory.

It became the beginning of a continuing life.

From that day forward, fearful disciples became bold witnesses, broken lives found restoration, and ordinary believers devoted themselves to the apostles' doctrine, fellowship, breaking of bread, and prayers. They lived as a people transformed by repentance, the saving name of Jesus Christ, and the abiding presence of the Holy Ghost.

Their story is now our invitation.

The chapters that follow trace that invitation through Scripture—from ancient promise to glorious fulfillment— revealing that the same Jesus who poured out His Spirit in Jerusalem continues to call men and women into a life of faith, holiness, worship, prayer, fellowship, and faithful witness.

For the story of Pentecost is not simply about what God did.

It is about what God is still doing.
