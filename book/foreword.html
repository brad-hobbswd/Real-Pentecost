Foreword

More Than a Moment

An invitation to rediscover the beauty, simplicity, and continuing life of biblical Pentecost.

Every generation inherits words that become so familiar they begin to lose their meaning.

Grace becomes a greeting instead of the undeserved favor of God.

Faith becomes optimism rather than complete dependence upon Jesus Christ.

Worship becomes a style instead of a response to divine revelation.

Pentecost has suffered the same fate.

For some, the word describes a denomination.

For others, it represents a style of worship, passionate preaching, or an extraordinary spiritual experience.

Entire conversations rise and fall upon personal memories, traditions, and assumptions until the original story becomes hidden beneath centuries of interpretation.

The Story Luke Tells

The book of Acts offers something remarkably simple.

Luke never attempts to define Pentecost through labels or movements.

He simply tells a story.

The story begins with a promise spoken by Jesus Christ.

One hundred twenty believers wait together in Jerusalem.

A sound from heaven fills the house.

The Spirit is poured out.

Peter preaches.

Three thousand repent, are baptized in the name of Jesus Christ, and receive the promise of the Holy Ghost.

Then Something Unexpected Happens

Luke stops describing the extraordinary and begins describing the ordinary.

People gather around tables.

They devote themselves to the apostles' doctrine.

They pray together.

They share their possessions.

They care for widows.

They encourage the weary.

They carry the gospel into cities, homes, prisons, and marketplaces.

The miracle becomes a way of life.

Why This Book Exists

That observation gave birth to this book.

These pages are not an attempt to defend a denomination or critique another tradition.

They are an invitation to walk slowly through the pages of Acts and ask one simple question.

What did Pentecost actually create?

The answer reaches far beyond a single day in Jerusalem.

Pentecost created a people shaped by repentance, identified with the saving name of Jesus Christ, filled with His Spirit, devoted to apostolic truth, transformed in character, sustained through prayer, united in fellowship, faithful in witness, and steadfast through every season of life.

Perhaps the greatest testimony of Pentecost is not found in the upper room at all.

Perhaps the greatest testimony is found in ordinary believers whose daily lives quietly reveal the continuing presence of Jesus Christ.

My hope is that these pages encourage every reader to look beyond labels and rediscover the beauty of the biblical pattern revealed in Scripture.

The story has never ended.

The same Jesus who poured out His Spirit in Jerusalem still calls people to repentance, still fills believers with the Holy Ghost, and still forms a people who continue steadfastly in the apostles' doctrine, fellowship, breaking of bread, and prayers.

Real Pentecost is more than a moment.
It is a life in the Spirit.

The invitation remains open.
