Foreword

Why This Journey Began

An invitation to rediscover the beauty and simplicity of biblical Pentecost.

As an educator, I have discovered that the most meaningful lessons are rarely the ones simply memorized. They are the truths that quietly reshape the way we think, influence the way we live, and remain with us long after the classroom has emptied.

Every day I watch students ask questions, wrestle with ideas, and experience the joy that comes when understanding finally replaces uncertainty. Those moments have continually reminded me that genuine learning is not merely about information—it is about transformation.

As a father, those lessons have become even more personal.

When I look at my son, I find myself thinking less about what I hope he will accomplish and more about the faith I hope he will embrace. More than success, recognition, or possessions, I pray that he will know Jesus Christ, love His Word, and walk daily in the power of the Holy Ghost.

I want him to inherit something that cannot fade—a faith rooted in Scripture, anchored in truth, and lived with humble obedience.

Those two callings, teaching and fatherhood, have continually brought me back to one simple question.

"What did the apostles actually teach?"

The answer is both beautifully simple and profoundly life-changing.

When I returned to the book of Acts with an open Bible and a willing heart, I discovered that Pentecost was never presented as an isolated event remembered only through history. It was the beginning of a people transformed by repentance, baptized in the saving name of Jesus Christ, filled with the Holy Ghost, and steadfastly devoted to the apostles' doctrine, fellowship, breaking of bread, and prayers.

That discovery became the foundation for this book.

Real Pentecost was written from a conviction that every generation deserves the opportunity to return to Scripture without assumption or tradition speaking louder than the Word of God itself. My desire is not to present a new idea but to rediscover an ancient one—the gospel faithfully preached by the apostles and centered completely upon Jesus Christ.

These pages are not an argument against sincere people who love God. Rather, they are an invitation to sit quietly with an open Bible and allow Scripture to tell its own story.

Along the way we will encounter promises spoken by the prophets, fulfilled by Jesus Christ, proclaimed by the apostles, and experienced by ordinary men and women whose lives were forever changed by the power of the Holy Ghost.

We will see that Pentecost was never intended to be a single extraordinary moment preserved in history. It became the beginning of a continuing life marked by repentance, worship, fellowship, holiness, prayer, faithful witness, and unwavering devotion to the apostles' doctrine.

My prayer is that readers will approach these chapters with humility, curiosity, and a sincere desire to know Jesus Christ more fully. Whether you have walked with Him for many years or are opening the Scriptures with fresh eyes, may His Word speak clearly and His Spirit lead faithfully.

The promise poured out in Jerusalem continues to call people today. It continues to transform lives, renew hearts, strengthen families, and build communities of believers devoted to Christ.

Perhaps that is why this journey matters so deeply to me—not only as an educator or as a father, but as a disciple continually learning that the greatest lessons are found at the feet of Jesus.

Every page that follows is simply an invitation to join that journey.
