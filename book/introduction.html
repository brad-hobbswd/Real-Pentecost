Introduction

More Than a Moment

Discovering the continuing life of Pentecost.

Pentecost is often remembered as a remarkable day in the history of the church. We read of a rushing mighty wind, tongues like fire, and disciples filled with the Holy Ghost, boldly declaring the wonderful works of God. It is a story that inspires awe and invites reflection.

Yet the book of Acts presents Pentecost as something far greater than a single extraordinary event. It marks the beginning of a new covenant community—a people transformed by Jesus Christ and empowered to live a continuing life of faith, worship, holiness, prayer, fellowship, and faithful witness.

That continuing life is the heart of this book.

The pages that follow are not intended to defend a tradition or promote a denomination. They are an invitation to return to Scripture and allow God's Word to reveal the beautiful simplicity of the apostolic message.

We will journey from ancient promises spoken by the prophets to their fulfillment in Jesus Christ. We will stand with the disciples in the upper room, listen to Peter's sermon on the Day of Pentecost, and follow the early believers as they continue steadfastly in the apostles' doctrine.

Along the way one truth becomes increasingly clear: Pentecost was never intended to remain a memory.

It was always meant to become a way of life.

The first believers understood that following Jesus Christ involved far more than embracing a new idea or participating in a single spiritual experience. They became a people whose lives were continually shaped by repentance, baptism in the name of Jesus Christ, the gift of the Holy Ghost, and unwavering devotion to the apostles' doctrine.

Their faith was visible in the way they worshiped, prayed, cared for one another, and proclaimed the gospel. They gathered with gladness and simplicity of heart, finding their identity not in culture or tradition but in the living Christ who had redeemed them.

More than two thousand years have passed, yet the same invitation remains. The promise spoken in Jerusalem has never lost its power. Jesus Christ continues to call ordinary people into extraordinary lives marked by grace, truth, and the abiding presence of the Holy Ghost.

This book is an invitation to slow down and read Scripture carefully. Rather than approaching familiar passages with preconceived conclusions, I invite you to consider the testimony of the prophets, the words of Jesus, and the witness of the apostles with fresh eyes.

Ask questions. Compare passages. Follow the unfolding story from promise to fulfillment. Allow God's Word to interpret God's Word, and trust that the same Spirit who inspired the Scriptures is faithful to illuminate them today.

Throughout these chapters we will discover that the message proclaimed at Pentecost was beautifully simple and wonderfully transformative. It called people to repentance, pointed them to the saving name of Jesus Christ, promised the gift of the Holy Ghost, and invited them into a continuing life devoted to truth, fellowship, prayer, holiness, and faithful witness.

"And they continued steadfastly in the apostles' doctrine and fellowship, and in breaking of bread, and in prayers."

Acts 2:42 (KJV)

That verse serves as the foundation for everything that follows. It is not merely a description of the early church but a picture of the continuing life God desires for every believer—a life centered upon Jesus Christ and sustained by His Spirit.

As you turn the page, my prayer is simple: that you will encounter the living Christ through His Word and discover that Pentecost is not merely something to be remembered.

It is a promise to be embraced, a truth to be lived, and a life to be continued.
