Frequently Asked Questions

Exploring Scripture one question at a time.

The purpose of these answers is not to replace personal Bible study but to encourage every reader to search the Scriptures and discover the beauty of God's Word for themselves.

Acts 17:11
"These were more noble... in that they received the word with all readiness of mind, and searched the scriptures daily, whether those things were so."

What is Pentecost?

Pentecost is the fulfillment of God's promise to pour out His Spirit, marking the beginning of the New Testament church and the continuing work of Jesus Christ among His people.

Why is Acts 2:38 important?

Peter's response to those seeking salvation includes repentance, baptism in the name of Jesus Christ, and the promise of receiving the gift of the Holy Ghost.

What is the apostles' doctrine?

The apostles' doctrine is the teaching faithfully delivered by Jesus Christ through His apostles and continued by the early church.

Who is the Holy Ghost?

The Holy Ghost is the promised Spirit of God, empowering believers to live faithfully, walk in truth, and bear witness to Jesus Christ.

Why is prayer essential?

Prayer is continual fellowship with God, shaping our hearts, strengthening our faith, and aligning our lives with His will.

What is biblical holiness?

Holiness is a life set apart for God, reflecting His character through obedience, love, worship, and daily faithfulness.

Why study Scripture?

God's Word reveals His character, teaches truth, corrects error, and equips believers for every good work.

Who is Real Pentecost for?

Real Pentecost is written for seekers, new believers, families, teachers, pastors, Bible study leaders, and anyone desiring a deeper understanding of Scripture.

Where should I begin?

Start with the Book, explore Acts 2:38 and Acts 2:42, continue through the chapters, and use the Study Guide to deepen your understanding.
