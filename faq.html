Frequently Asked Questions

Exploring Scripture one question at a time.

Every sincere question presents an opportunity to return to God's Word. These answers are intended to encourage thoughtful Bible study, personal reflection, and a deeper understanding of Jesus Christ and the continuing life of the Spirit.

Acts 17:11
"These were more noble... in that they received the word with all readiness of mind, and searched the scriptures daily, whether those things were so."

What is Pentecost?

Pentecost is the fulfillment of God's promise to pour out His Spirit and the beginning of the New Testament church.

Why is Acts 2:38 important?

Peter's response reveals repentance, baptism in the name of Jesus Christ, and the promise of the Holy Ghost.

What is Acts 2:42?

The early believers continued steadfastly in the apostles' doctrine, fellowship, breaking of bread, and prayers.

What is the Holy Ghost?

The promised Spirit of God dwelling within believers, empowering them to live faithfully and bear witness to Jesus Christ.

Why is prayer important?

Prayer is continual communion with God, strengthening faith and shaping our lives according to His will.

What is holiness?

Holiness is a life set apart for God, reflecting His character through obedience, worship, and love.

What is the apostles' doctrine?

The teaching delivered by Jesus Christ through His apostles and faithfully continued by the early church.

Why study Scripture?

God's Word reveals truth, strengthens faith, corrects error, and equips believers for every good work.

Who is Real Pentecost for?

Anyone seeking a deeper understanding of Scripture, whether reading individually, with family, or studying in a church community.
